About Me

I am a Robotics Ph.D. student at Carnegie Mellon University with a passion for space exploration. As a member of the Robomechanics Lab, I am engaged in research with my advisor Professor Aaron Johnson to develop robotic climbing capabilities for planetary rovers.

I recently graduated from Franklin W. Olin College of Engineering, where I received a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering. Thanks to Olin's project-focused curriculum, I have had many opportunities to pursue hands-on engineering projects, several of which are displayed on this website. These experiences have given me valuable practice at problem-solving, self-direction, and collaboration, and allowed me to apply the concepts I learned in class to actual engineering challenges.

I am also interested in teaching, and at Olin I served as a course assistant for classes like Quantitative Engineering Analysis, Partial Differential Equations, and Transport Phenomena. In my free time I enjoy playing board games, reading books, and playing the trombone.
